There are drawbacks to using BMI to assess women's health.

BMI is a hotly debated tool, with many health professionals believing it should be abolished. In the 18th century, when BMI was first created, it relied heavily on data gleaned from the remains of the dead.

Dempers points out that the average height and weight of both sexes have risen since then.

A sportsperson

In general, the more physically active you are, the more muscle mass you tend to accumulate. "According to Dempers, "BMI does not come into consideration muscle mass or body fat, hence it is worthless for athletes."

According to Mcdonough, muscles with a greater density also weigh more. "As a consequence, BMI measurements are distorted. "The vast majority of athletes are likely to be overweight or obese."

Postmenopausal

'The older you are, the more body fat you tend to accumulate,' explains Mcdonough. "Hormone levels fall in women going through menopause. Muscle mass declines and abdominal fat rises as a result of a drop in hormone levels."
